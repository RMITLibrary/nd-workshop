				<h3 id="top-slide1" tabindex="-1">Reference</h3>
				<p>Your annotated bibliography should begin with a reference of the text that you are annotating. Check your assignment instructions to see which referencing style you need to use. If you are unsure, ask your instructor. Refer to <a href="https://learninglab.rmit.edu.au/referencing/" target="_top">Referencing</a> for more detail, examples and activities. Visit <a href="https://www.lib.rmit.edu.au/easy-cite/" target="_top">EasyCite</a> for a guide to referencing in the most commonly used styles at RMIT.<br>
					Here's an example of a reference for a journal article using the APA 7th edition style:</p>
					<blockquote><p><strong>Example reference</strong></p>
						<p>Kim, S.G., &amp; Singh, Q.I. (2022). Working remotely: Current trends and challenges, <em>Journal of Current Management Theory</em>, <em>27</em>(12), 83-96. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.cmt/2022.11.19</p>
					</blockquote>

					<h3 id="top-slide2" tabindex="-1">What to include in a summary</h3>
					<p>When writing an annotated bibliography, begin with a summary for each of the texts you have read. As you read, take notes in your own words about:</p>
					<ul>
					<li>the main aim or argument</li>
					<li>the supporting arguments</li>
					<li>how the study was conducted (the methodology)</li>
					<li>the main findings</li>
					</ul>
					<p>This will help you write a clear 50–100-word paragraph.</p>

				<h3 id="top-slide4" tabindex="-1">What to include in a critical analysis</h3>
				<p>If your assignment guide requires you to include a critical analysis, you will need to focus on the value of the material and sources you are looking into. Consider asking yourself:</p>
				<ul>
				<li>What are the strengths and limitations of the text in terms of aim, methodology, and findings?</li>
				<li>Are the findings logical and well researched?</li>
				<li>Is the text original, important and of a high standard?</li>
				<li>How does this text add to the research in the field?</li>
				<li>How does it fit into and relate to the broader field of research and academic discussions?</li>
				</ul>
				<p>The example on the following slide shows how a student has critically analysed an article to identify strengths, limitations and possible weaknesses.</p>

				<h3 id="top-slide6" tabindex="-1">What to include in a reflection</h3>
				<p>Writing a reflection for your annotated bibliography includes writing a few sentences explaining why or why not the source text is useful or helpful for your research and/or how it relates to the overall topic of your research.</p>
				<p>Reflective writing involves sharing your personal thoughts and assessments, but it should still be clear and unemotional. </p>
